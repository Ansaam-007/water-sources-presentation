Surface Water

  • Rivers & streams — flowing water fed by rain and snowmelt
  • Lakes & reservoirs — stored water used for drinking and irrigation
  • Wetlands — filters pollutants, supports biodiversity
  • Glaciers & snowpack — long-term frozen storage (shrinking globally)
Slide 4 of 7
🪨

Groundwater

  • Aquifers — underground rock layers holding water
  • Wells — drilled to access aquifer water directly
  • Springs — natural discharge points at the surface
  • Supplies ~30% of all freshwater used globally
Slide 5 of 7

Other Water Sources

🌧️ Rainwater Harvesting

Collecting precipitation from rooftops and surfaces for local use.

🌊 Desalination

Removing salt from seawater — energy-intensive but increasingly common.

♻️ Recycled Water

Treated wastewater reused for irrigation, industry, and sometimes drinking.

🌫️ Atmospheric Water

Fog collectors and air-to-water generators extract moisture from the air.

Threats to Water Sources

  • Pollution — industrial runoff, pesticides, plastics
  • Over-extraction — depleting aquifers faster than they recharge
  • Climate change — altering rainfall patterns and melting glaciers
  • Population growth — increasing demand on limited supply

Key Takeaways

  • Freshwater is scarce — only a tiny fraction is accessible
  • Surface water and groundwater are the primary sources
  • Alternative sources like desalination are growing in importance
  • Conservation and protection are urgent global priorities
